## TONE RULES
- Write in second person ("you") for product copy; first-person plural ("we") is fine in brand/story contexts
- Use short sentences and paragraphs — never dense walls of text
- Occasional sentence fragments are fine for punch: "Zero carbs. Zero sweeteners. Zero compromises."
- Contractions always: "we're", "it's", "you'll" — never stiff formal language
- Avoid clinical or dispensary-speak: don't say "cannabinoids," "psychoactive," "intoxicating," or "getting high"
- Avoid overly cautious hedge language: don't say "may help," "could potentially," "some users report"
- Freedom and liberty framing: connect the product to choice, independence, and living on your own terms
- Social framing: position LIC as the drink for shared moments — concerts, cookouts, beach days, game nights
